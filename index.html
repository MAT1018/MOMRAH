About Us

The Ministry of Municipalities and Housing (MoMaH) is a key government entity in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, responsible for urban development, housing, and municipal services across the nation. Our mission is to create sustainable and vibrant communities that enhance the quality of life for all citizens and residents.

Our Mission

To lead the development of municipal and housing sectors through innovative policies, strategic partnerships, and effective governance, in line with Saudi Vision 2030.

Our Journey

Established in 1975, the Ministry has been at the forefront of urban transformation in the Kingdom. We have launched numerous initiatives that have reshaped the urban landscape and provided housing solutions for millions of Saudis.

Our Work in Action

UN Collaboration

The Ministry of Municipalities and Housing collaborates with United Nations agencies to drive sustainable urban development and housing initiatives in Saudi Arabia. Our partnerships with UNDP and UN-Habitat focus on knowledge exchange, capacity building, and implementing global best practices.

United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)

Our partnership with UNDP focuses on sustainable urban development, climate resilience, and innovation in municipal services. Key initiatives include:

  • Smart City Solutions for Urban Challenges
  • Capacity Building for Municipal Staff
  • Sustainable Housing Policies and Practices

United Nations Human Settlements Programme (UN-Habitat)

Collaboration with UN-Habitat emphasizes urban planning, affordable housing, and inclusive city development. Our joint projects include:

  • National Urban Development Strategies
  • Community Engagement in Urban Planning
  • Innovative Solutions for Affordable Housing

Sakani Housing Support Program

Providing affordable housing solutions for Saudi families

About the Program

The Sakani Housing Support Program is a flagship initiative of the Ministry of Municipalities and Housing, designed to facilitate homeownership for Saudi citizens. The program offers financial assistance, subsidized mortgages, and a range of housing options to meet diverse family needs.

Key Features

  • Financial support for down payments and construction
  • Partnerships with real estate developers for affordable housing units
  • Online platform for housing services and applications
  • Customized solutions for different income levels

Bahja Urban Revitalization

Transforming historic districts into vibrant cultural hubs

Project Overview

Bahja is an urban revitalization initiative that transforms historic neighborhoods into vibrant cultural and commercial centers while preserving their heritage. The project focuses on enhancing public spaces, improving infrastructure, and creating opportunities for local businesses.

Key Components

  • Restoration of historic buildings and landmarks
  • Development of pedestrian-friendly public spaces
  • Support for local artisans and small businesses
  • Integration of modern amenities with traditional architecture

CamCom AI Visual Pollution Project

Using artificial intelligence to enhance urban aesthetics

Innovative Urban Management

CamCom is an AI-powered system that detects and manages visual pollution in urban environments. Using computer vision and machine learning, the system identifies issues such as unauthorized advertisements, graffiti, and poorly maintained facades, enabling efficient urban management.

Technology Components

  • AI-powered image recognition system
  • Mobile app for citizen reporting
  • Automated workflow for issue resolution
  • Data analytics for urban planning
  • Integration with municipal enforcement systems
